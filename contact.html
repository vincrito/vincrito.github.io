Appointment Request

How to Request an Appointment

To schedule a consultation, please contact our office directly by phone or email using the information on this page. We respond to all appointment requests within one business day.

When reaching out, it is helpful to have the following available:

  • Type of visit (new patient, second opinion, or provider referral)
  • Primary diagnosis or reason for consultation
  • Referring provider name and contact information, if applicable
  • Any prior treatment history relevant to your case

For medical emergencies, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

Before Your Visit

Records to Send in Advance

To make your consultation as productive as possible, please arrange to send your records 48–72 hours before your appointment. Our team will coordinate record requests with you.

Imaging

  • CT scan with contrast (CD or digital)
  • MRI / MRCP if obtained
  • PET scan if performed
  • All radiology reports

Pathology & Labs

  • Biopsy or surgical pathology reports
  • Molecular / genomic profiling results
  • Relevant blood work (tumor markers, LFTs)

Clinical Records

  • Consultation notes from other providers
  • Prior operative reports
  • Endoscopy, ERCP, or EUS reports

Other

  • Complete medication list
  • Insurance card (front and back)
  • Photo ID
