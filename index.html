Camilo Correa, MD

Most patients don’t expect to be thinking about their pancreas or liver. And when something shows up: a cyst, a mass, an abnormal scan, it often comes out of nowhere. If you’ve landed here, you’re probably dealing with something involving the pancreas, liver, or another complex abdominal condition. These diagnoses often come with a lot of uncertainty, and the amount of information online can make things more confusing rather than less. What follows is usually a flood of information: scans, reports, appointments, and many opinions.

I’m a cancer surgeon focused on pancreatic, liver, and upper gastrointestinal diseases. I spend most of my time helping patients sort through these situations; figuring out what matters, what doesn’t, and what needs to be done next. One of the most common misconceptions is that every finding needs to be treated aggressively. In reality: Some problems need surgery Some are better treated with medications or other therapies Some are safest to watch over time Getting that distinction right is the most important part. This website is an extension of the conversations I have with patients every day. You’ll find: Straightforward explanations of common diagnoses How we think about treatment decisions What different paths (surgery, surveillance, systemic therapy, radiation therapy) actually mean Practical guidance on what questions to ask My goal is not just to treat disease, but to help patients understand what’s happening and why certain decisions are made. If you’re here because of a new diagnosis or an ongoing issue, this is a good place to start.
